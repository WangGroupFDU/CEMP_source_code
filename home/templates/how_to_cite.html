Citation Guide

How to Cite CEMP and Its Modules

If you use any functionality of the CEMP platform, the CEMP platform article must be cited. If your work additionally uses specific modules such as Polymer, Ionic Liquid, or Autocompute, please also cite the corresponding module-specific publications listed below.

Mandatory Citation for CEMP

If CEMP is used in your work, the following arXiv article is a required citation.
Wang, Jifeng; Ju, Jiazhe; Wang, Ying. CEMP: a platform unifying high-throughput online calculation, databases and predictive models for clean energy materials. arXiv preprint arXiv:2507.04423, 2025.
https:

Additional Module-Specific Citations

The following references are additionally required when the corresponding module has been used in your study.

  • Polymer Wang, J.-F.; Sun, Y.-B.; Chen, Q.-T.; Ji, F.-F.; Song, Y.-Y.; Ruan, M.-Y.; Wang, Y. OpenPoly: a polymer database empowering benchmarking and multi-property predictions. Chinese J Polym Sci 2025, 43 (10), 1749-1760.
  • Ionic Liquid Wang, J.; Wang, Y. BRICS-Based Generation and AI-Assisted Screening of Ionic Liquids with Mechanistic Insights into Lithium Transport in Electrolytes. Journal of Chemical Information and Modeling 2025, 65 (20), 10961-10976. DOI: 10.1021/acs.jcim.5c01824.
  • Autocompute Wang, J.; Wang, Y. Strategies to improve the quantum computation accuracy for electrochemical windows of ionic liquids. The Journal of Physical Chemistry B 2024, 128 (8), 1943-1952.
