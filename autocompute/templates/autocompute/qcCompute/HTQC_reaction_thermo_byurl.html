    1. Introduction to the High-Throughput Reaction Thermodynamic Properties Calculation Module

    This module facilitates high-throughput quantum chemistry calculations based on Gaussian16, incorporating both structure optimization and single-point energy calculations.

  • Structure Optimization:
  • Theoretical level: B3LYP/6-311G**
  • Dispersion correction: GD3BJ
  • Frequency correction factor: 0.9682
  • The program automatically checks for optimization failures or imaginary frequencies and performs error correction if necessary.
  • Single-Point Energy Calculation:
  • Theoretical level: M062X/6-311+(2d,p)
  • Dispersion correction: GD3
  • Implicit solvent model: SMD (Simulating the reaction environment, such as solvent conditions, using the static dielectric constant.)
  • The program automatically checks for calculation failures and performs error correction if necessary.
    • Users need to download the provided Excel file, fill in the calculation parameters, and upload the completed file. The program will automatically execute the calculations and download the results upon completion.

  • The current calculation parameters are only suitable for organic molecular systems.
  • Ensure the system does not contain transition metals, as this could lead to erroneous results.

    • 3. Calculation Details

    1. Calculation of Enthalpy Change (ΔH):

    \[ \Delta H = \sum_{\text{products}} \nu_i H_i^\circ - \sum_{\text{reactants}} \nu_j H_j^\circ \] where:
    • \( \nu \): Stoichiometric coefficients of the substances.
    • \( H^\circ \): Standard enthalpy of the substances.

    2. Calculation of Gibbs Free Energy Change (ΔG):

    \[ \Delta G = \sum_{\text{products}} \nu_i G_i^\circ - \sum_{\text{reactants}} \nu_j G_j^\circ \] where:
    • \( G^\circ \): Standard Gibbs free energy of the substances.

    3. Relation between ΔG, ΔH, and ΔS:

    \[ \Delta G = \Delta H - T \Delta S \] where:
    • \( \Delta S \): Entropy change of the reaction.
    • \( T \): Absolute temperature (in Kelvin).

    By utilizing quantum chemistry calculations, we can accurately determine the thermodynamic properties of chemical reactions. These calculations are essential for predicting reaction behavior and guiding experimental and industrial applications.

    How to Fill the EXCEL Spreadsheet for Reaction Thermodynamics Calculation

    Users can fill multiple Reactant and Product columns. For each reactant, ensure the column names are in the form: Reactant_Name_{N}, Reactant_SMILES_{N}, Reactant_Coefficient_{N}. For the same reactant, N must be the same. Similarly, for products, you should fill in Product_Name_{N}, Product_SMILES_{N}, Product_Coefficient_{N}. Here, Reactant_Coefficient_{N} and Product_Coefficient_{N} are the reaction coefficients; you can fill in any numerical values (according to the actual chemical reaction equation). The eps column represents the reaction environment; when users are reacting under a specific solvent, the eps column should be filled with the static dielectric constant of that solvent. If the reaction takes place in the gas phase, eps should be filled with 0. Users can fill in any number of reactant or product columns.
