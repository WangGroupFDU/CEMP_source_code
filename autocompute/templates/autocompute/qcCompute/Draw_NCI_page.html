    This module implements the Non-Covalent Interaction (NCI) analysis to visualize and qualitatively classify weak interactions. It evaluates the reduced density gradient s and constructs isosurfaces in regions of low electron density (ρ) and small gradients; surfaces are colored by sign(λ2)·ρ to indicate interaction character—blue (negative) for attractive contacts (e.g., hydrogen bonds), green (near zero) for dispersive/van der Waals regions, and red (positive) for steric repulsion.
    Two density sources are supported: self-consistent densities (Gaussian .fchk/.cub; ORCA .molden/.cube) and the promolecular approximation (requiring only atomic species and coordinates) for rapid screening of large systems.
    Upon completion, the workflow outputs rendered NCI isosurfaces (.tga), volumetric grids (s.cube and sign(λ2)ρ.cube), and optional RDG scatter plots and summary statistics (.csv/.png), together with reproducibility scripts.
    Note: For quantitative comparison or fine discrimination, self-consistent densities are recommended; promolecular results are intended for qualitative prescreening.
